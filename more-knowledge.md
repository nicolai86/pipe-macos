Geometric Primitive Recovery (Stock Detection)Because STL files lack native topology, the system must reconstruct the stock type using Normal Vector Distribution analysis.Rectangular HSS DetectionHeuristic: Triangle normals cluster into four primary orthogonal directions (excluding end caps).Constraint: If $>90\%$ of surface normals occupy $<10$ discrete angular bins, classify as Rectangular.Validation: Verify via Oriented Bounding Box (OBB) dimensions.Round & Oval (HSS-O) DetectionHeuristic: A continuous gradient of normals exists around a central axis.Round: Use a 2D Hough Transform on cross-section slices to detect a single radius.Oval: Detect two distinct radii using a least-squares cylinder fit on the major and minor axes.2. Miter and Cutout RecognitionTo detect features on a "dumb" mesh, the parser must identify planar intersections and manifold boundaries.Miter Detection (Plane Fitting)Algorithm: Use RANSAC (Random Sample Consensus) to identify the largest planar surfaces at the mesh extremities ($X_{min}$ and $X_{max}$).Calculation: If the plane normal is not parallel to the longitudinal axis, a miter exists. The angle $\theta$ is calculated relative to the tube axis.Cutout ExtractionEdge Analysis: Identify Boundary Edges (edges shared by only one triangle).Loop Extraction: Chain these edges into closed loops.Classification: * Loops at the absolute $X$ extents are End Cuts.Closed loops within the $X$ range are Feature Cutouts.3. Rotary Kinematics & TransformationThe software must translate 3D coordinates into 4-axis G-code ($X, Y, Z, A$).Cylindrical Unwrapping (Round Stock)To convert the $Y$ coordinate of the 3D model into a rotary $A$ position:$$A = \frac{Y}{R} \cdot \frac{180}{\pi}$$Where $R$ is the outer radius of the HSS.Four-Face Mapping (Rectangular Stock)Indexing: The machine indexes to $0^\circ, 90^\circ, 180^\circ,$ and $270^\circ$.Corner Interpolation: To maintain Constant Tip-to-Work Distance, the $Z$ and $A$ axes must interpolate simultaneously when traversing the corner radii.4.
